Section 002

The Problem Statement

DeFi Wealth Remains Disconnected from Real-World Impact

The total value locked (TVL) in DeFi protocols has surpassed $100 billion at peak, yet less than 0.01% of this wealth has been systematically directed toward humanitarian causes. The crypto narrative has been dominated by speculation, yield farming, and meme coins — creating enormous wealth for early participants while the communities most in need remain entirely unaffected. This disconnect represents not just a missed opportunity, but a moral failure of the ecosystem.

According to the UN Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA), global humanitarian funding requirements exceeded $55 billion in 2025, with a funding gap of approximately $22 billion. Meanwhile, cryptocurrency market capitalization exceeded $3 trillion. The math is undeniable — even a fractional reallocation could close the humanitarian funding gap entirely.

Traditional Charity is Broken by Design

The traditional charitable giving model suffers from four fundamental flaws that have persisted for decades: Opacity — donors rarely know exactly how their money is spent, with administrative overhead often consuming 20-40% of funds; Intermittency — giving is event-driven (natural disasters, holiday seasons) rather than continuous, creating feast-or-famine cycles for NGOs; Friction — the process of donating is cumbersome, requiring multiple steps and often involving credit card fees that further erode the intended impact; and Verification Gap — there is no standardized, immutable proof that funds were used for their stated purpose.

A 2024 study by the Charity Navigator Foundation found that only 23% of donors felt "very confident" that their donations were used effectively. This trust deficit directly suppresses giving — potential donors hold back precisely because they cannot verify impact.

High Barriers to Entry Exclude the Many

Early-stage token opportunities in DeFi are overwhelmingly captured by venture capital firms, insider networks, and individuals with significant capital and technical expertise. The average person in a developing nation — the very demographic that humanitarian aid is designed to serve — has virtually zero access to these wealth-generation mechanisms. This creates a perverse irony: the people who need financial empowerment most are the least able to access it.

Humanity Coin's $6 entry fee is deliberately calibrated to be accessible across global income levels. At $6, the barrier is low enough for participation in over 90% of global markets while still providing meaningful commitment filtering to prevent sybil attacks and bot exploitation.

Crypto Volatility Destroys NGO Planning Ability

When NGOs do receive cryptocurrency donations (which is rare), they face a critical problem: volatility. An NGO that receives $50,000 in a volatile token may find its purchasing power reduced to $35,000 within days, making budgeting for long-term projects impossible. Humanity Coin solves this through its Stable Payout mechanism — all vault disbursements are instantly converted to stablecoins (USDT/USDC) before reaching NGO wallets, ensuring that the full intended value is preserved.

Section 003

The Claim Entry Model

The Claim Entry Model is Humanity Coin's innovative approach to democratizing early-stage token access. Unlike traditional presales, IDOs, or ICOs — which favor the wealthy and well-connected — our model ensures that anyone, anywhere, can participate with minimal friction while maintaining robust security against exploitation.

1

Join the Ecosystem

Users access the Humanity Coin claim interface through our web application or integrated partner platforms. The onboarding process has been meticulously designed to require fewer than 60 seconds from start to finish. Users connect a compatible Web3 wallet (MetaMask, Trust Wallet, WalletConnect, or our native built-in wallet) and complete a basic identity verification tier that prevents mass bot registration while preserving privacy.

Anti-Sybil Protection

Each claim is tied to a unique wallet address combined with device fingerprinting and IP range analysis. Duplicate claims from the same identity cluster are automatically flagged and rejected by our middleware layer before reaching the smart contract.

2

Pay the $6 Nominal Entry Fee

A one-time, non-refundable entry fee of $6 (payable in BNB or USDT) grants the user access to a fixed-price token allocation valued at $1,000. This fee serves multiple critical functions: it provides a commitment signal that filters out casual browsers and bots; it generates initial liquidity that is added directly to the DEX pool; and it creates a psychological anchoring effect that increases long-term holder retention rates.

Economic Rationale

The $6 fee was determined through extensive modeling across 140+ global markets. At this price point, participation is accessible to individuals earning as little as $2/day (representing 3 days' wages), while still generating sufficient aggregate capital to seed liquidity. Our simulations project 500,000+ claims in the first 90 days, yielding $3M+ in initial liquidity provisioning.

3

Claim $1,000 Fixed-Price Allocation

Upon successful payment, the user's wallet is credited with a $1,000 valued allocation of $HC tokens at the fixed claim price. This allocation is not immediately transferable — it enters a structured vesting period (see Step 4) designed to prevent dump dynamics, protect market price stability, and align user incentives with the long-term health of the ecosystem. The fixed-price mechanism ensures that every participant, regardless of when they claim during the public window, receives identical terms.

Fair Launch Guarantee

Unlike tiered presale models that allocate more tokens to larger contributors, Humanity Coin's claim model is deliberately egalitarian. Every wallet receives the same $1,000 allocation at the same fixed price, with no VIP tiers, no priority queues, and no insider preferential pricing.

4

90-Day Linear Vesting Period

The claimed allocation vests over a 90-day linear schedule, releasing approximately 1/90th of the total allocation daily. This mechanism is critical for several reasons: it prevents a massive sell pressure event on Day 1 (which would crash the price and harm all participants); it creates a natural holding incentive where the best financial outcome comes from remaining engaged with the ecosystem; and it allows the DEX liquidity pool to mature organically as trading volume builds gradually.

During the vesting period, users still earn 4% reflection rewards on their fully vested balance, meaning even locked tokens generate passive yield. Additionally, referral rewards ($HC from successful referrals) are not subject to vesting — they are instantly available, creating a powerful incentive to share the project during the vesting window.

Section 005

The Proof of Impact Protocol

The Proof of Impact Protocol (PoIP) is Humanity Coin's proprietary three-step verification system that ensures every dollar distributed from the Humanity Vault creates verifiable, documented real-world impact. This is not aspirational — it is mechanical and enforced at the smart contract level.

Step 1 of 3

Aid Portal — Vetted NGO Funding Requests

Only NGOs that have passed our rigorous due diligence process (see Section 10: Security & Compliance) can submit funding requests through the Aid Portal. Each request must include: a detailed project description with measurable outcomes; a line-item budget breakdown showing exactly how funds will be used; a timeline with specific milestones; and the target beneficiary count. The portal automatically formats these requests into standardized governance proposals that the DAO community can evaluate and vote on.

Example: "WaterAid Kenya requests $45,000 from the Humanity Vault to construct 3 borehole wells in Turkana County, serving approximately 4,500 people. Project duration: 90 days. Milestones: Site survey (Day 15), Drilling complete (Day 45), Pump installation and testing (Day 75), Community handover (Day 90)."

Step 2 of 3

Impact Verification — On-Chain Evidence

Upon receiving funds, NGOs are contractually required to upload verification evidence to the blockchain at each milestone. This evidence includes: photographic documentation with embedded EXIF GPS data and timestamps; digital receipts for all expenditures; third-party verification from local community leaders or independent auditors; and beneficiary count confirmation. All evidence is stored on-chain via IPFS integration, making it permanently accessible, tamper-proof, and publicly auditable.

NGOs that fail to provide verification evidence within the specified milestone windows are automatically flagged by the protocol. Repeated failures trigger a DAO vote for potential delisting, which would permanently remove the NGO's ability to request future funding from the Humanity Vault.

Step 3 of 3

Stable Payouts — Zero Volatility for NGOs

When the DAO approves a funding disbursement, the $HC tokens in the Humanity Vault are not sent directly to the NGO. Instead, they are automatically routed through our integrated DEX aggregator and converted to USDT or USDC at the best available market rate. Only the stablecoin output reaches the NGO's wallet. This eliminates the volatility risk that has historically made cryptocurrency donations impractical for organizations that need predictable budgets. An NGO approved for $50,000 in funding receives exactly $50,000 in stablecoins — not a penny less due to market fluctuations.

The Humanity Vault Architecture

Vault Security Layers

  • Timelock: All disbursements are subject to a 48-hour timelock, giving the community time to review and potentially veto any suspicious transaction.

  • Multi-Sig: The vault requires 3-of-5 signatories for any disbursement above $10,000, with signatories drawn from the core team, independent auditors, and community-elected delegates.

  • Daily Limits: Maximum daily disbursement capped at 5% of total vault balance to prevent catastrophic drain scenarios.

  • Emergency Pause: DAO can trigger a 72-hour vault freeze if suspicious activity is detected, requiring a supermajority (67%) vote to lift.

  • Audit Trail: Every vault interaction (deposit, conversion, disbursement) emits detailed events that are indexed and displayed on our public Impact Dashboard.

Section 010

Security & Compliance

Tiered KYC Framework

Progressive identity verification based on interaction level

Tier 0 (under $500 in total withdrawals) requires only wallet connection and basic device verification — no personal documents. Tier 1 (withdrawals between $500 and $5,000) requires government-issued ID verification through our integrated KYC partner. Tier 2 (withdrawals exceeding $5,000) requires enhanced due diligence including proof of address and source of funds documentation. This tiered approach ensures that the vast majority of users in developing nations can participate without facing documentary barriers, while still maintaining robust anti-money laundering (AML) controls at higher transaction volumes that could attract regulatory scrutiny.

Withdrawal Queue System

Smart management for the 90-day unlock event

When the 90-day vesting period concludes, a large number of users will simultaneously become eligible to withdraw their fully vested tokens. Without management, this could create gas wars on the BSC network, extreme slippage on the DEX, and a poor user experience. Our Withdrawal Queue system addresses this through a randomized, staggered withdrawal window. Users are assigned a specific 6-hour window within a 7-day withdrawal period, during which they can withdraw at standard gas rates. This distributes the sell pressure across time, protecting both the token price and the user experience.

DAO Governance Framework

Humanity Coin is governed by its community of token holders through a structured DAO framework. Governance power is directly proportional to $HC holdings, ensuring that those with the greatest stake in the ecosystem have the greatest influence over its direction.

Governable Parameters

  • NGO Funding Approval: Vote on individual funding requests from the Aid Portal

  • Tax Rate Adjustment: Propose changes to the 10% transaction tax split (within ±2% bounds)

  • NGO Whitelisting/Delisting: Add or remove NGOs from the eligible partner list

  • Vault Emergency Actions: Trigger or lift vault freeze in response to security events

  • Protocol Upgrades: Approve smart contract upgrades through timelocked execution

Voting Mechanism

Quorum Requirement

Minimum 15% of total circulating supply must participate for a vote to be valid. This prevents small minorities from passing proposals when the majority is inactive.

Passing Threshold

Standard proposals require simple majority (>50%). Emergency actions (vault freeze, contract pause) require supermajority (67%). Constitutional changes require 75%.

Voting Period

Standard proposals: 5-day voting window. Emergency proposals: 24-hour accelerated window with 48-hour execution timelock.

Delegation

Holders can delegate their voting power to trusted community members or governance specialists without transferring token ownership.
